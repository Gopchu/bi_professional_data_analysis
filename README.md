# Data Professional Survey Breakdown
## Project Objective
In this project, we will analyze the results of our employee survey, which contains data on employee salaries, job satisfaction, and overall satisfaction with their current working arrangements.

## Dataset Used
- <a href="https://github.com/Gopchu/bi_professional_data_analysis/blob/main/Power%20BI%20-%20Final%20Project.xlsx">Survey Data<a/>

## Questions

-The analysis primarily focuses on how salary, in relation to job title, influences employee satisfaction.

## Process

-This process includes cleaning the data, ensuring that the correct data types are used, removing unnecessary columns, and preparing the dataset for effective visualization.

## Analysis

-We begin by presenting the geographic distribution of our employees by country of origin, along with the total number of survey responses and the average age of the participants. These metrics provide a solid foundation for the analysis.


![Image_1](https://github.com/Gopchu/bi_professional_data_analysis/blob/main/Image_1.png)  

![Image_2](https://github.com/Gopchu/bi_professional_data_analysis/blob/main/Image_2.png)  

-As shown in the visuals, our workforce is highly diverse, with the majority of employees originating from countries within the Anglosphere. 
-With an average age of 29.87 years, our employees represent a relatively young workforce. 


-Next, we examine how salaries vary across different job titles.
-Additionally, we analyze how salary differs by sex to identify any potential disparities in employee compensation.


![Image_3](https://github.com/Gopchu/bi_professional_data_analysis/blob/main/Image_3.png)  

-The visualizations present a positive picture of the company's compensation structure. Salaries appear to align appropriately with job responsibilities, with Data Scientists receiving the highest average compensation.  
-Based on the salary distribution by sex, there is no evidence of a significant gender pay gap. The data suggests that compensation is driven primarily by role and performance rather than sex.  

![Image_4](https://github.com/Gopchu/bi_professional_data_analysis/blob/main/Image_4.png)  

-As shown by the gauge charts, employees reported neutral to slightly positive levels of satisfaction with their workload and its impact on work-life balance.  
-However, salary satisfaction tells a different story. The gauge leans slightly toward the negative side, suggesting that employees are generally less satisfied with their current compensation than with other aspects of their work.  

![Image_5](https://github.com/Gopchu/bi_professional_data_analysis/blob/main/Image_5.png)  

-We also analyzed employees' preferred programming languages to better understand their technical interests and career aspirations.  
-This analysis provides insight into the skills and technologies that employees are most interested in developing.  
-As the results show, Python is the preferred programming language for the majority of employees.  
-At the same time, the smaller groups interested in other programming languages should not be overlooked. Supporting these interests may create opportunities for career development, role transitions, or promotions into positions that better align with employees' skills and ambitions.  

## Conclusion  

-In conclusion, the dashboard indicates that most employees are generally satisfied with their work-life balance, reflecting a positive workplace culture. However, salary satisfaction is noticeably lower, suggesting that compensation remains a key area for improvement. To increase overall employee satisfaction and strengthen retention, the company should consider reviewing its compensation strategy. In addition, providing clear opportunities for promotions, career progression, and role transitions that align with employees' skills and interests may further enhance job satisfaction and long-term employee engagement.
